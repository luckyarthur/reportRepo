# James J. Hill — From Telegraph Operator to "Empire Builder" of the Great Northern Railway

> **A note before you start.** The man this report is about — James J. Hill, the railroad builder — is *not* the same person as Napoleon Hill, the author of *Think and Grow Rich*. They share a surname; that is the only relation. Napoleon Hill writes *about* James J. Hill as one of his examples; he is not writing about himself.

## 1. Background of the story

James J. Hill (1838–1916) was born in rural Ontario, Canada, to a modest family. He lost his father at the age of 14 and left school to support his mother. In 1856 — at age 18 — he moved south of the border to St. Paul, Minnesota, and went to work on the Mississippi River wharves as a shipping clerk. There he learned the unsentimental economics of long-distance transport at the ground level: tonnage, rates, routes, seasons, ice, freight contracts. It was the kind of operational education no university offered.

The America of Hill's working years was being stitched together by transcontinental railroads, most of them built on the back of enormous federal land grants. Promoters would take the grant, push the rails into a wilderness, sell the bonds, and let the operations be somebody else's problem. The result was a string of overbuilt, undermaintained, frequently bankrupt railroads. James J. Hill's eventual road — the Great Northern Railway — was the rare major transcontinental built **without** government subsidy. That single structural choice made him one of the most respected industrialists of the era and earned him the nickname *"the Empire Builder."*

Napoleon Hill uses James J. Hill in *Think and Grow Rich*, especially in Chapter 6 (Organized Planning) and Chapter 8 (Persistence), as the textbook example of a man who, when his first plan failed, did not quit — he simply built a *new* plan and tried again. Napoleon Hill writes — in the chapter on Persistence — that James J. Hill **"met with temporary defeat when he first endeavored to raise the necessary capital"** to build a railroad from the East to the West. He did not interpret that defeat as permanent. He replanned, and tried again.

## 2. Life path of the main role

- **Clerk on the Mississippi wharves.** Hill started in the steamboat and freight business in St. Paul. He learned the economics of long-distance transport from the inside.
- **Telegraph operator.** He acquired the communications skill — and the network of contacts up and down the river — that would later let him coordinate an entire railroad.
- **Coal and fuel business.** In St. Paul, Hill built a sizeable coal and fuel operation. It produced both capital and operational reputation, two things he would need on a much larger scale shortly.
- **St. Paul & Pacific Railroad.** In the late 1870s Hill and a small syndicate bought the bankrupt St. Paul & Pacific. He reorganized it, extended it, and rebranded it into a viable enterprise.
- **Temporary defeat raising capital.** Napoleon Hill explicitly cites this stretch — Hill the railroad man "met with temporary defeat when he first endeavored to raise the necessary capital." Most men, faced with the same defeat, would have quit. James J. Hill built *new* plans and tried again.
- **Building the Great Northern.** Across the 1880s and into the 1890s, Hill drove rails west across the Dakotas, Montana, Idaho, and Washington — completing the Great Northern Railway to the Pacific in **1893**, without federal land grants.
- **The "Empire Builder."** Hill became one of the most respected industrialists in America and one of its wealthiest men. He continued to run, expand, and reorganize railroads — including major interests in the Northern Pacific — into the early twentieth century.

## 3. How he became rich — main factors

- **Organized Planning.** Each leg of construction was preceded by detailed surveying, traffic studies, and *settlement plans*. Hill literally helped settle the American Northwest by promoting immigration along his line — farms along the route would produce freight to carry. The plans were not just engineering; they were demand-creation as well.
- **Persistence through replanning.** Napoleon Hill highlights him as the canonical case of "*temporary defeat is not permanent failure.*" When the first capital-raising plan failed, he did not abandon the goal — he built a new plan and tried again. The willingness to *replan* is the hidden form of persistence; most quitters mistake a bad plan for a bad goal.
- **Specialized knowledge stacked over decades.** Shipping clerk, telegraph operator, coal merchant, railroad reorganizer, surveyor's client, financier — by the time Hill attempted the transcontinental, he had operational fluency in every input the project required. The stack made him uniquely credible.
- **Vertical integration of demand.** He did not just build track. He recruited immigrant farmers to settle the land along the route, knowing that without freight to carry, the rails would be empty. Building demand alongside supply is one of the great underrated wealth strategies of the nineteenth century — and the twenty-first.
- **Quality over subsidy.** The Great Northern was built to higher engineering standards than its subsidized rivals — gentler grades, more direct routes, better track. When the **Panic of 1893** wiped out heavily subsidized competitors with overbuilt and poorly routed lines, Hill's railway survived and absorbed business its rivals could no longer carry. Cheaper-to-build competition went bankrupt; Hill's higher-quality line stayed open.
- **Definiteness of purpose held over almost two decades.** From the bankrupt St. Paul & Pacific to the Pacific Ocean was, in operational terms, nearly twenty years of patient, plan-revising work toward one chief aim.

## 4. What we can learn — especially about getting rich

The single most quotable line about James J. Hill comes from Napoleon Hill: *"Temporary defeat is not permanent failure. It may only mean that your plans have not been sound. Build other plans. Start over again."* That is the entire James J. Hill operating system in three sentences. The hidden mistake most people make at the first defeat is to treat it as a verdict on the goal. James J. Hill treated it as a verdict on the plan. The goal stayed; the plan got rewritten.

A second lesson is the **skill stack.** Hill spent years as a shipping clerk, then a telegraph operator, then a coal merchant, then a railroad reorganizer. By the time he attempted the transcontinental, he was the most credible operator alive at every single input the project required. The implication for modern careers is that staying inside one adjacent industry for two decades, deliberately stacking the skills you need, builds the kind of operational credibility that lets you attempt projects nobody else can credibly attempt.

A third lesson is **demand creation.** Hill did not assume the freight would show up. He recruited the freight. The modern translation is exact — every founder, every salesperson, every author who has worried about distribution should notice that the answer is not always "wait for the market." Sometimes the answer is "go settle the prairie." Build the demand for what you sell.

A fourth lesson is **quality as a long-run wealth strategy.** Hill's competitors built faster and cheaper, and many of them went bankrupt in 1893. Hill's higher-spec railway survived the downturn and absorbed the failed lines around it. In a long enough timeframe, operational quality is the cheapest moat available. The savings from cutting corners disappear in the first real downturn.

Finally, the most important meta-lesson is the time horizon. James J. Hill held one chief aim — to build a sound transcontinental railroad — across almost two decades. Most people abandon goals on a timescale shorter than the goals require to actually pay. Hold your chief aim long enough for the breakthrough to become obvious in retrospect; almost everybody quits before then.

### Lessons for getting rich today

- "*Temporary defeat is not permanent failure. It may only mean that your plans have not been sound. Build other plans. Start over again.*" Memorize it.
- Stack adjacent specialized knowledge for a decade or two until you are the most credible operator in your industry at every input the big project will require.
- Build demand for what you sell — don't assume the market exists. Settle the prairie alongside the rails.
- Out-quality the subsidized competition. Cheaper-built rivals go bankrupt in the first real downturn; the high-quality operator survives and absorbs them.
- Hold one chief aim across decades. Most people abandon goals on a timescale far shorter than the timescale on which goals actually pay.
- When a plan fails, ask "is the *goal* wrong, or is the *plan* wrong?" Almost always it is the plan. Replan and try again.

# 中文版本

# James J. Hill(詹姆斯·J·希尔)—— 从电报员到 Great Northern Railway(大北方铁路)的"帝国建造者"

> **开始之前请注意。** 本报告写的是 James J. Hill —— 那位铁路建设者 —— *不*是《Think and Grow Rich》的作者 Napoleon Hill。两人姓氏相同,关系仅止于此。Napoleon Hill 是在自己的书里把 James J. Hill *作为例子*来谈的;他并不是在写自己。

## 背景

James J. Hill(1838–1916)出生于加拿大 Ontario(安大略)的乡村,一户普通人家。14 岁丧父,他辍学供养母亲。1856 年 —— 18 岁那年 —— 他南渡至 Minnesota(明尼苏达州)St. Paul(圣保罗),在密西西比河码头当航运职员。他在那里从最底层学到了长途运输那种毫不感伤的经济学:吨位、运价、航线、季节、冰情、货运合同。这是任何大学都不提供的实操教育。

Hill 工作时期的美国,正被一条条横贯大陆的铁路缝合在一起,其中绝大多数都是靠巨额联邦土地赠予建起来的。投机者拿到土地、把铁轨推进荒野、卖出债券,然后让运营变成别人的麻烦事。结果是一串过度建设、维护不足、屡屡破产的铁路。James J. Hill 最终的那条线 —— Great Northern Railway(大北方铁路)—— 是少数主要的、**没有**政府补贴的横贯大陆铁路之一。这一项结构选择本身就让他成为那个时代最受尊敬的工业家之一,并为他赢得绰号 *"the Empire Builder"*(帝国建造者)。

Napoleon Hill 在《Think and Grow Rich》里,尤其是第六章(Organized Planning,有组织的规划)和第八章(Persistence,坚持)里,把 James J. Hill 当作教科书式的例子:一个第一份计划失败后没有放弃、只是另起*一份新计划*的人。Napoleon Hill 在坚持那一章写道,James J. Hill 在最初试图为修建一条从东到西的铁路 **"筹集所需资金时遭遇了暂时性的失败"(met with temporary defeat when he first endeavored to raise the necessary capital)**。他没有把这次失败解读为终局。他重新做计划,然后再试一次。

## 主角的人生轨迹

- **密西西比码头的职员。** Hill 在 St. Paul 从蒸汽船和货运生意入行。他从内部学到了长途运输的经济学。
- **电报员。** 他获得了通信技能 —— 以及顺河上下的人脉网络 —— 这些后来让他能协调起一整条铁路。
- **煤炭与燃料生意。** 在 St. Paul,Hill 建起了一份规模可观的煤炭与燃料生意。它同时产生资本和运营声誉,这两样东西他不久后会在更大规模上需要。
- **St. Paul & Pacific Railroad(圣保罗与太平洋铁路)。** 1870 年代末,Hill 和一个小财团买下了破产的 St. Paul & Pacific。他重组了它,扩展了它,把它打造成一家可行的企业。
- **筹资时的暂时性失败。** Napoleon Hill 明确指出这一段 —— 铁路人 Hill"在最初试图筹集所需资金时遭遇了暂时性的失败"。换作大多数人,在同样的挫败面前早就放弃。James J. Hill 另起*新计划*,继续干。
- **建造 Great Northern。** 整个 1880 年代直到 1890 年代,Hill 一路把铁轨向西推过 Dakotas(达科他)、Montana(蒙大拿)、Idaho(爱达荷)和 Washington(华盛顿州) —— 在 **1893 年**把 Great Northern Railway 修到了太平洋,而且没有联邦土地赠予。
- **"Empire Builder"。** Hill 成为美国最受尊敬的工业家之一,也是最富有的人之一。直到二十世纪初,他都还在经营、扩张、重组铁路 —— 包括对 Northern Pacific(北太平洋铁路)的重大权益。

## 致富之路与主要因素

- **有组织的规划(Organized Planning)。** 每一段建设都先经过详尽的勘测、客货量研究和*移民安置计划*。Hill 实际上是通过推动沿线移民,亲自帮忙开拓了美国西北部 —— 沿线农场会产生可以装车的货物。这些计划不止是工程,也是需求创造。
- **通过重新规划实现的坚持。** Napoleon Hill 把他选作"*暂时性失败不是永久性失败*"(temporary defeat is not permanent failure)的标准案例。当第一份筹资计划失败时,他没有放弃目标 —— 他另起新计划,再试一次。"愿意重新规划"是坚持的隐藏形式;大多数放弃者把坏计划误以为是坏目标。
- **跨数十年叠加的专门知识。** 航运职员、电报员、煤炭商、铁路重组者、勘测雇主、融资人 —— 到 Hill 着手横贯大陆铁路时,这项目所需要的每一项输入,他都已运营上熟练。这种"技能栈"让他的可信度独一无二。
- **需求的垂直整合。** 他不只是铺铁轨。他招募移民农户去沿线定居,因为他知道没有货运的铁轨就是空线。在供给侧之外同步建设需求,是十九世纪 —— 也是二十一世纪 —— 最被低估的致富策略之一。
- **以品质替代补贴。** Great Northern 的工程标准比那些拿补贴的对手要高 —— 坡度更缓、线路更直、轨道更优。当 **1893 年大恐慌(the Panic of 1893)** 扫掉那些过度建设、走向歪斜的拿补贴对手时,Hill 的铁路活下来,并接收了对手承担不了的业务。建得便宜的对手破产了;品质更高的他活了下来。
- **目的的明确性持续了将近二十年。** 从破产的 St. Paul & Pacific 一路到太平洋,在运营层面上是近二十年朝同一主要目标的耐心、不断改写计划的工作。

## 我们能学到什么 —— 尤其是关于致富

关于 James J. Hill 最值得引用的一句来自 Napoleon Hill:"*Temporary defeat is not permanent failure. It may only mean that your plans have not been sound. Build other plans. Start over again.*"(暂时性的失败不是永久性的失败。它可能只意味着你的计划不健全。另起新计划,从头再来。) 这三句话就是 James J. Hill 的整套操作系统。多数人在第一次失败时犯的隐性错误,是把它当作对*目标*的判决。James J. Hill 把它当作对*计划*的判决。目标留下来;计划被改写。

第二条教训是**技能栈(skill stack)**。Hill 当了多年航运职员,然后电报员,然后煤炭商,然后铁路重组者。到他着手横贯大陆铁路时,这项目所需的每一项输入,他都是当时活着的人里最可信的运营者。对现代职业的启示是,在一两个相邻行业里待上二十年,有意识地叠加你需要的技能,会构筑起那种"让你能去尝试别人不可信地尝试不了的项目"的运营信用。

第三条教训是**创造需求**。Hill 没有假设货物会自动出现。他亲自把货物招募进来。现代翻译完全对应 —— 每一位创始人、销售员、作者,只要担心过分销,都该意识到答案不总是"等市场来"。有时候答案是"去开拓那片草原"。为你所卖的东西去创造需求。

第四条教训是**把品质作为长期致富战略**。Hill 的对手们建得更快、更便宜,1893 年时许多对手都破产了。Hill 那条规格更高的铁路撑过了下行,并把周围倒下的线路一并吸收。在足够长的时间尺度上,运营品质是最便宜的护城河。"偷工减料"省下的钱,会在下一次真正的衰退里清零。

最后,最重要的元教训是时间尺度。James J. Hill 把一个主要目标 —— 修建一条稳固的横贯大陆铁路 —— 坚持了近二十年。多数人放弃目标的时间尺度,远短于这些目标真正开始回报所需要的时间尺度。把你的主要目标坚持得足够久,直到突破事后看来理所当然;几乎所有人都在那之前就放弃了。

### 今日致富的清单

- "*暂时性的失败不是永久性的失败。它可能只意味着你的计划不健全。另起新计划,从头再来。(Temporary defeat is not permanent failure. It may only mean that your plans have not been sound. Build other plans. Start over again.)*" 背下来。
- 在相邻行业里把专门知识叠加十几二十年,直到你成为大项目所需的每一项输入上最可信的运营者。
- 为你所卖的东西创造需求 —— 别假设市场已经存在。在铁轨旁边把草原开拓出来。
- 在品质上打败拿补贴的对手。建得便宜的对手会在下一次真衰退中破产;高品质的运营者活下来并把他们吸收。
- 把一个主要目标坚持几十年。多数人放弃目标的时间尺度,远短于这些目标真正开始回报所需的时间尺度。
- 当一份计划失败时,先问"是*目标*错了,还是*计划*错了?" 几乎总是计划错了。改计划,再试一次。
