# Edwin C. Barnes — The Man Who "Thought" Himself into Partnership with Edison

## 1. Background of the story

In the early years of the twentieth century, the United States was being electrified, and a single man — Thomas A. Edison — sat at the center of it. From his laboratories in West Orange and East Orange, New Jersey, Edison was already a household name: inventor of the phonograph, the practical incandescent lamp, the motion picture camera, and an entire industrial research model that other inventors would copy for a century. To work for Edison was a privilege. To become his business *partner* — equal letters on a contract, equal share of the profits — was, on paper, fantasy.

Napoleon Hill opens *Think and Grow Rich* with the story of Edwin C. Barnes precisely because the fantasy came true. Barnes is the very first illustration in the book, deliberately chosen as the cleanest possible demonstration of "the secret." That secret, named over and over in Chapter 1, is *Definite Desire*: a burning, particular, decided wish — not a hope, not a wish — backed by a refusal to consider any alternative. Barnes' life, as Hill tells it, is Step One of the entire philosophy made flesh.

## 2. Life path of the main role

Barnes arrived in Orange, New Jersey as a young man with no money, no introduction, and no obvious qualification for what he wanted. He had ridden a freight train "blind baggage" — the period slang for stowing away — because he could not afford a passenger ticket. When he presented himself at Edison's laboratory he looked, in Edison's own later recollection, "like an ordinary tramp."

Yet Edison hired him. Edison, who had become a careful judge of men, said later that something about the man's face announced "he had made up his mind to get what he had come after." Barnes was put to work at the most menial available job, at nominal wages — sweeping floors and running errands inside the empire he intended to co-own.

Then the years passed. One year. Two. Three. Four. Five. To any outside observer Barnes was simply a low-level employee whose ambition had quietly collapsed into the routine. He himself saw it differently; inside his own mind, he records, he was already Edison's partner, waiting only for the world to recognize a fact he had already settled.

The opening came in the form of a machine no one wanted. Edison had perfected a dictating machine — what would be marketed as the "Ediphone." Edison's salesmen were unimpressed; they considered it unsellable. Barnes seized it. He sold it so successfully that Edison gave him a national distribution contract under a label that read **"Made by Edison and Barnes."** The signage of the partnership Barnes had visualized for half a decade was now literally printed on the product.

## 3. How he became rich — main factors

- **A definite, burning DESIRE.** Hill is specific: Barnes did not *hope* to work with Edison. He had *decided* to be Edison's partner. The decision preceded the means.
- **Burning the bridges behind him.** A blind-baggage freight ride is a one-way ticket. Barnes left himself no plausible fallback to retreat to.
- **A specific, clearly visualized goal.** Not "a job at Edison's lab," not "to get into electronics" — but *partner with* Edison, named, dated, decided.
- **Willingness to start at any wage, in any job, that put him in proximity.** Proximity, not title, was the prize. Sweeping floors at Edison's place beat managing somewhere else.
- **Persistence across five visible years of no progress.** From the outside it looked like nothing was happening. From the inside, the partnership was being assembled.
- **Spotting and seizing the one opening others dismissed.** The Ediphone was the unfashionable product. Salesmen will not touch what they think will fail; Barnes saw what was actually a vehicle waiting for the right driver.
- **Acting as if the partnership were already real.** Hill: "HE WAS THE PARTNER OF EDISON EVERY MINUTE." Behavior followed identity, not the other way around.

## 4. What we can learn — especially about getting rich

Most people fail at this very first step because they confuse a wish for a decision. A wish is reversible; it asks for permission from circumstance. A decision is not. Until you have decided — irrevocably — that you are going to become whatever the thing is, you have not begun.

The practical, modern translation of Barnes' method is not mystical:

1. **Write the goal down** — definite sum, definite role, definite date.
2. **Cut off Plan B.** Quit the safe job. Move to the city. Spend the savings on the equipment. Whatever your "freight train" is, ride it.
3. **Get physically and professionally close to the opportunity.** Take the unglamorous role inside the right company over the prestigious role inside the wrong one.
4. **Expect a long, invisible interval.** Five years of nothing visible is normal. The world's bookkeeping runs slow.
5. **Adopt the identity now.** You are the partner / founder / millionaire today. Outward circumstances are bookkeeping.

The danger Hill warns against — "Plan B" thinking — is more important than it sounds. A retreat path is not a safety net; it is a permission slip to give up. Barnes had no permission slip. The mind that knows it cannot retreat becomes extraordinarily inventive about going forward.

### Lessons for getting rich today

- Write your "definite chief aim" down in a single sentence with a number and a date — and re-read it twice a day.
- Burn at least one bridge: quit, move, sign the lease, take the loan. Decisiveness is a verb.
- Position yourself geographically and professionally next to the opportunity, even if it means a lower title and a lower wage.
- Take the unfashionable assignment that nobody else wants — it is almost always the one with an open path to the top.
- Plan for the five-year silent stretch where nothing seems to be moving. That is the work.
- Behave, dress, speak, and decide today as the person you intend to be — Barnes was "the partner of Edison every minute," long before the contract.

# 中文版本

# Edwin C. Barnes（埃德温·C·巴恩斯）—— 那个"想"出与 Edison（爱迪生）合伙的人

## 背景

二十世纪初的美国正处在电气化浪潮中,而站在这个浪潮中心的,是一个人 —— Thomas A. Edison（托马斯·A·爱迪生）。在他位于新泽西州 West Orange（西奥兰治）和 East Orange（东奥兰治）的实验室里,Edison 早已家喻户晓:留声机、实用白炽灯、电影摄影机的发明者,以及一种被往后一个世纪不断模仿的工业研究模式的开创者。能在 Edison 手下工作已是莫大荣幸;成为他的*生意合伙人* —— 合同上并列署名、利润对等分配 —— 在纸面上几近痴人说梦。

Napoleon Hill（拿破仑·希尔）之所以把 Edwin C. Barnes 的故事放在《Think and Grow Rich》（《思考致富》)的开篇,正是因为这个"痴人说梦"实现了。Barnes 是全书的第一个例证,被精心挑选出来作为对"那个秘密"最干净的演示。这个在第一章被反复点名的秘密,就是 **明确的渴望(Definite Desire)**:一种灼热的、具体的、已经下定决心的愿望 —— 不是希望,不是奢望 —— 并且拒绝考虑任何替代方案。Hill 笔下 Barnes 的一生,就是整套哲学第一步的活体呈现。

## 主角的人生轨迹

Barnes 抵达新泽西 Orange 时,是一个没有钱、没有引荐、也没有任何明显资格的年轻人。他是搭货运火车"blind baggage"(扒车,当时的行话)过来的 —— 他买不起客车票。当他出现在 Edison 的实验室门口时,用 Edison 后来的回忆原话说,他"看起来像个普通的流浪汉(like an ordinary tramp)"。

然而 Edison 雇了他。Edison —— 这位早已是看人能手的发明家 —— 后来说,这个人的脸上有种东西宣告着"他已经下定决心要得到他来这里寻求的东西(he had made up his mind to get what he had come after)"。Barnes 被分配去做能找到的最低贱的工作,拿名义工资 —— 在他打算共同拥有的帝国内部扫地、跑腿。

然后,时间过去了。一年。两年。三年。四年。五年。在任何外人眼里,Barnes 只是个低级雇员,他的雄心已经悄悄塌陷在日常琐事里。但他自己看法不同;在他心里,他记录道,他早已是 Edison 的合伙人,只等世界承认一个他已经认定的事实。

机会以一种没人想要的机器的形式出现。Edison 完善了一台口述记录机 —— 后来取名"Ediphone"。Edison 的销售员对此无动于衷,觉得它根本卖不出去。Barnes 抓住了它。他卖得非常成功,以至于 Edison 把全国分销合同交给了他,产品上印着的标签是:**"Made by Edison and Barnes"(爱迪生与巴恩斯出品)**。Barnes 在脑中幻想了五年的合伙招牌,现在真的印在了产品上。

## 致富之路与主要因素

- **一个明确而灼热的渴望(DESIRE)。** Hill 说得很清楚:Barnes 没有*希望*为 Edison 工作。他已经*决定*要成为 Edison 的合伙人。决定先于手段。
- **烧掉退路。** 扒货车是单程票。Barnes 没给自己留任何可信的退路。
- **一个具体、清晰可视的目标。** 不是"在 Edison 实验室找份工作",也不是"进入电子行业" —— 而是*与* Edison *合伙*,有名有姓、有日期、已决定。
- **愿意从任何工资、任何工作做起,只要它能让自己接近机会。** 重要的是物理接近,而不是头衔。在 Edison 那里扫地胜过在别处当主管。
- **在外界看不到任何进展的五年间持续坚持。** 从外面看什么也没发生。从里面看,合伙关系正在被一点一点搭建。
- **发现并抓住别人不屑一顾的那个机会。** Ediphone 是不时髦的产品。销售员不会碰他们觉得会失败的东西;Barnes 看到的,其实是一辆等待合适司机的车。
- **当下就以合伙人的身份行动。** Hill 写道:"HE WAS THE PARTNER OF EDISON EVERY MINUTE"(他每一分钟都是 Edison 的合伙人)。行为追随身份,而不是反过来。

## 我们能学到什么 —— 尤其是关于致富

绝大多数人在这第一步就失败,因为他们把愿望误当成决定。愿望是可逆的;它向境遇请求许可。决定不是。在你 —— 不可挽回地 —— 决定要成为某种人之前,你根本还没开始。

Barnes 方法的现代实用翻译并不神秘:

1. **把目标写下来** —— 具体金额、具体角色、具体日期。
2. **切断 Plan B(后备方案)。** 辞掉安稳的工作。搬到那个城市去。把积蓄花在装备上。你的"货运火车"是什么,就去坐它。
3. **在物理上和职业上靠近机会。** 在对的公司做不光鲜的角色,胜过在错的公司做光鲜的角色。
4. **预期一段漫长且无形的间隔期。** 五年外界看不到任何动静是正常的。世界的账本走得很慢。
5. **现在就采用那个身份。** 你今天就是合伙人/创始人/百万富翁。外部境遇只是延迟入账的会计科目。

Hill 警告的"Plan B 思维"危害比听起来更严重。退路并不是安全网,它是一张允许你放弃的许可证。Barnes 没有这张许可证。一个知道自己无法撤退的大脑,会变得极其善于想办法前进。

### 今日致富的清单(Lessons for getting rich today)

- 把你"明确的主要目标(definite chief aim)"用一句话写下来,带数字、带日期 —— 每天读两遍。
- 至少烧掉一座桥:辞职、搬家、签下租约、贷款。果断是一个动词。
- 把自己在地理上和职业上摆到机会旁边,即便意味着头衔更低、薪水更低。
- 接下别人都不想做的"不光鲜"任务 —— 它几乎总是通向顶端的开放赛道。
- 为那段看似毫无动静的五年沉默期做好计划。那才是真正的工作。
- 今天就以你想成为的那个人的方式去言行、决策 —— Barnes "每一分钟都是 Edison 的合伙人(was the partner of Edison every minute)",早在合同签下之前就是了。
