# Task: Edwin C. Barnes — The Man Who "Thought" Himself into Partnership with Edison

Source: *Think and Grow Rich!* by Napoleon Hill (Introduction & Chapter 1: Desire).

Write `output.md` in this folder. The report must cover, in this order, the following four sections.

## 1. Background of the story
- Set the scene: where and when Barnes' desire first formed (early 1900s, USA; Edison's laboratories at West Orange / East Orange, New Jersey).
- Who Thomas A. Edison was at that point in history, why becoming his business associate (not employee) was extraordinarily ambitious, and what "the secret" of *Think and Grow Rich* is being illustrated through Barnes (Definite Desire / Step 1).

## 2. Life path of the main role
- Barnes as an unknown young man with no money and no introduction to Edison.
- The decision to travel "blind baggage" on a freight train to Orange, NJ.
- The first meeting with Edison — Barnes looking "like an ordinary tramp" but mentally a king.
- Being hired at the most menial job at nominal wages.
- The five-year wait, during which no outsider saw any progress.
- The launch of the Edison Dictating Machine ("Ediphone") that no Edison salesman would touch — Barnes took it, sold it, and finally got his partnership ("Made by Edison and Barnes").

## 3. How he became rich — main factors
List the principles Hill explicitly credits, and explain each in 2-4 sentences:
- A definite, burning DESIRE (not a hope, not a wish).
- Burning the bridges behind him (one-way freight train, no fallback plan).
- A specific, clearly visualized goal — *partner with* Edison, not work *for* him.
- Willingness to start at any wage doing any job that put him in proximity to the opportunity.
- Persistence across five visible years of no progress.
- Spotting and seizing the one opening (the Dictating Machine) that others dismissed.
- Acting as if the partnership were already real ("HE WAS THE PARTNER OF EDISON EVERY MINUTE").

## 4. What we can learn — especially about getting rich
- Why most people fail at this step: they wish instead of decide.
- Concrete, modern-day actions: write down a definite financial/career goal, commit irrevocably, position yourself physically and professionally next to opportunity, work the unglamorous job that nobody else wants, expect to wait years before the visible breakthrough.
- The mindset shift: you are the partner / the millionaire / the founder *now*; outward circumstances catch up later.
- The danger of "Plan B" thinking and how cutting off retreat focuses the mind.

## Style

- Quote 2–3 short lines directly from Hill (e.g., Edison's own quoted recollection).
- End with a 4–6-bullet "Lessons for getting rich today" checklist.
