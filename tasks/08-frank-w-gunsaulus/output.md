# Dr. Frank W. Gunsaulus — The "Million-Dollar Sermon" and the Birth of Armour Institute

## 1. Background of the story

In the late nineteenth century the South Side of Chicago — a stockyards district, dense, immigrant, industrial — was where a young clergyman named Frank Wakeley Gunsaulus (1856–1921) preached. Gunsaulus wanted something far larger than a successful pulpit. He carried inside him a vivid picture of a new kind of college, one in which young men and women would be taught *"to learn by doing"* — not by rote, not by orthodox curriculum, but by working with hands and minds together. It was a vision a century ahead of its time.

There was a problem the size of a city. He needed **one million dollars** — and in the nineteenth century that was roughly tens of millions of dollars in today's purchasing power. Gunsaulus was a young preacher with no fortune of his own, no wealthy patrons, no obvious way of raising even a fraction of the figure. So he did what most people do when faced with an enormous gap between dream and means: he wished. For two full years.

Napoleon Hill returns to this story in Chapter 5 of *Think and Grow Rich*, under the heading *"What I Would Do If I Had a Million Dollars."* He uses it to demonstrate Imagination — and, just as much, the kind of decision that turns imagination into cash.

## 2. Life path of the main role

Gunsaulus' first two years on the project were, in Hill's blunt phrase, fruitless. Every night he took the thought to bed; every morning he picked it up again. He prayed, he planned in his head, he refined the vision. "*But he had done nothing but think.*"

Then, on one specific Saturday afternoon, the act everyone else neglects took place: he reached a **definite decision.** He decided he would raise the million dollars — not someday, not when conditions improved, but **within one week.** He had no idea how. The how was not his concern; the what and the when were.

He called the newspapers. He told them that the next morning he would preach a sermon entitled *"What I Would Do If I Had a Million Dollars."* The phone call was the second decision — and it was, in Hill's framing, the bridge-burning act. After that announcement there was no graceful retreat.

That evening he wrote the sermon. It came easily, because he had been preparing it inside his own head for almost two years. The next morning, in the pulpit, he reached into his pocket and found that he had forgotten the manuscript at home. He closed his eyes and preached from the heart.

When he finished, a man rose from the third row from the back, walked to the pulpit, extended his hand, and said:

> *"I liked your sermon. I believe you can do everything you said you would if you had a million dollars. If you will come to my office tomorrow morning, I will give you the million dollars. My name is Phillip D. Armour."*

The next day the money was delivered. Gunsaulus founded the **Armour Institute of Technology** — which, after a 1940 merger, becomes today's **Illinois Institute of Technology (IIT).** Hill places exceptional weight on a single timing fact: **the money arrived within 36 HOURS of the moment he reached a definite decision.**

## 3. How he became rich (in vision and influence) — main factors

Gunsaulus did not "become rich" in the personal sense most of *Think and Grow Rich* describes. He raised — and deployed — a fortune in the service of an institution. The principles, however, are the same ones the book's millionaires applied to their own balance sheets.

- **Definite decision with a deadline.** "Within a week" turned two years of drift into 36 hours of result. Hill is unambiguous that the deadline did the heavy lifting; the dream alone had not.
- **Definiteness of Purpose.** The vision was not "a better educational system." It was a specific college, with a specific pedagogy, training a specific kind of student. Specific visions raise money; vague ones don't.
- **A burning desire that had matured over two years.** The readiness was already there. What had been missing was the act of deciding.
- **Imagination + Faith.** Hill places this story in the Imagination chapter precisely because Gunsaulus could *see* himself already in possession of the million dollars before going to sleep that Saturday night.
- **Auto-suggestion.** Two years of nightly incubation had rewritten his subconscious until the request felt natural, confident, and even casual. By Sunday morning he was not "trying" to ask for a million dollars. He was simply describing what he intended to do with one.
- **Public commitment.** The phone call to the newspapers made retreat impossible. The burned bridge multiplied his commitment.
- **Action congruent with the goal.** A clergyman *preached.* He used the only tool he commanded — the pulpit — but he used it at its absolute fullest. He did not wait to invent a new medium.

## 4. What we can learn — especially about getting rich

The Gunsaulus story is the cleanest single illustration in *Think and Grow Rich* that **the act of deciding — with a date attached — often unlocks resources that years of wishing cannot.** Two years of thinking produced nothing. A decision plus a deadline plus a public commitment produced **$1 million in 36 HOURS.** The numerator changed almost nothing; the denominator changed everything. The bottleneck was never the dream. The bottleneck was the decision.

A second, equally portable lesson is that **you do not have to know "how" before you can decide on "what" and "when."** Gunsaulus did not have a fundraising plan when he picked up the phone to call the newspapers. The how arrived after the decision, not before. Most people invert this — they refuse to decide until they know how — and so they never decide.

A third lesson is to **use the medium you already command.** Gunsaulus was a preacher; he preached. He did not wait until he had founded a magazine, or hired a fundraising consultant, or built a board of trustees. He took the tool that was already in his hand and used it at its fullest. The modern translation is exact: use the sermon, the code, the article, the sales call, the meeting you already have on the calendar — used at its absolute best, your existing medium is almost always sufficient.

A fourth lesson is **public commitment as bridge-burning.** Telling the newspapers was not marketing; it was psychological irreversibility. Once it was in the papers, the only paths forward were preaching and succeeding, or preaching and failing publicly. Failing privately was off the menu. That subtle change in the menu is what produces the change in behavior.

A fifth lesson is that **people fund visions, not requests.** Phillip D. Armour did not write a check because Gunsaulus *needed* a million dollars. He wrote a check because the sermon was about what would be *done* with a million dollars. Always sell the use, not the ask.

### Lessons for getting rich today

- Decide with a deadline. A decision plus a date plus a public commitment is the single highest-leverage act available to anyone.
- Stop trying to know "how" before deciding "what." The how arrives after the decision, not before. **Hill's lesson: the money arrived within 36 HOURS.**
- Use the medium you already command. Don't wait to invent a new one — preach the sermon, ship the code, write the article, take the meeting.
- Burn a small bridge by going public. Tell the newspapers — i.e., your peers, your customers, your team. Retreat becomes psychologically expensive.
- Speak from complete clarity about what you will *do* with the money, the role, the company. People fund visions, not requests.
- Incubate the dream for as long as it takes to feel natural — and then act in a single weekend, not in another two years.

# 中文版本

# Dr. Frank W. Gunsaulus(弗兰克·W·冈索鲁斯博士)—— "百万美元的布道"与 Armour Institute(阿莫尔理工)的诞生

## 背景

十九世纪末的芝加哥南区 —— 牲畜交易场所、密集、移民聚集、工业气息浓重 —— 是一位名叫 Frank Wakeley Gunsaulus(1856–1921)的年轻牧师讲道的地方。Gunsaulus 想要的远不只是讲台上的成功。他心里有一幅生动的图景:一所新型大学,让年轻男女"*在做中学*(to learn by doing)" —— 不靠死记、不靠正统课程,而是手与脑一起动起来。这是一种领先时代将近一百年的设想。

可问题大得像一座城。他需要**一百万美元** —— 而在十九世纪,这大约相当于今天几千万美元的购买力。Gunsaulus 是个没有任何个人财富的年轻牧师,没有富有的恩主,没有任何明显的办法去募集哪怕这数目的一小部分。所以他做了大多数人在面对"梦想与手段之间巨大裂口"时会做的事:他许愿。整整许了两年。

Napoleon Hill 在《Think and Grow Rich》第五章回到这故事,题为 *"What I Would Do If I Had a Million Dollars"*(《如果我有一百万美元,我会怎么做》)。他用它来演示想象力 —— 同样,也用它来演示那种把想象力变成现金的"决定"。

## 主角的人生轨迹

Gunsaulus 在这个项目上的头两年,用 Hill 直白的话说,毫无成果。他每晚带着这个念头入睡,每天清晨又把它捡起来。他祷告、在脑中规划、不断打磨愿景。"*但他除了想之外什么也没做。(But he had done nothing but think.)*"

然后,在某个具体的星期六下午,别人都忽略的那个动作发生了:他做出了一个**明确的决定(definite decision)**。他决定要筹到一百万美元 —— 不是"将来某一天",不是"等条件好转时",而是 **一周之内(within one week)**。他完全不知道怎么做。"怎么做"不归他管;"是什么"和"什么时候"才归他管。

他打电话给报社。他告诉他们,第二天早晨他会以 *"What I Would Do If I Had a Million Dollars"* 为题做一场布道。这通电话是第二个决定 —— 而且,在 Hill 的框架里,它是烧桥的动作。这条公告一出,优雅的退路就没有了。

那天晚上他写下这篇布道。写得很轻松,因为他已经在脑子里准备了近两年。第二天早晨,他在讲台上,伸手摸口袋,发现自己把讲稿落在家里了。他闭上眼,从心里讲了出去。

讲完时,从后排第三排站起一个人,走到讲台前,伸出手,说:

> *"我喜欢你的布道。我相信你说要做的那些事,如果你有一百万美元,你都能做到。如果你明天上午到我办公室来,我把这一百万美元给你。我叫 Phillip D. Armour。"("I liked your sermon. I believe you can do everything you said you would if you had a million dollars. If you will come to my office tomorrow morning, I will give you the million dollars. My name is Phillip D. Armour.")*

第二天钱到位。Gunsaulus 成立了 **Armour Institute of Technology(阿莫尔理工学院)** —— 经 1940 年合并,成了今天的 **Illinois Institute of Technology(伊利诺伊理工学院,IIT)**。Hill 把异常的份量压在一个时间事实上:**这笔钱在他做出明确决定之时起的 36 小时内(36 HOURS)就到位了。**

## 致富之路(就视野与影响力而言)与主要因素

Gunsaulus 没有以《Think and Grow Rich》多数描述的"个人致富"意义"变富"。他是在为一家机构服务的过程中,募集并部署了一笔财富。然而,他用的原则,和书里那些百万富翁用在自己资产负债表上的,完全是同一套。

- **带截止日期的明确决定。** "一周之内"把两年的漂浮变成了 36 小时的结果。Hill 毫不含糊:抬起重物的是那个截止日期;光有梦想抬不起来。
- **目的的明确性(Definiteness of Purpose)。** 那愿景不是"一个更好的教育体系"。它是一所具体的大学,有具体的教学法,培养具体类型的学生。具体的愿景能募到钱;模糊的不能。
- **酝酿了两年的灼热渴望。** 准备已经在那里。缺的是"做决定"这个动作。
- **想象力 + 信念。** Hill 把这故事放进想象力一章,正因为 Gunsaulus 在那个星期六晚上入睡之前,就已经能*看见*自己已拥有那一百万美元。
- **自我暗示(Auto-suggestion)。** 两年每晚的孵化,已经把他的潜意识改写到"提出请求"感觉自然、自信、甚至随意的程度。到星期天早晨,他不是在"努力"开口要一百万美元。他只是在描述,自己打算拿这一百万做什么。
- **公开承诺。** 那通打给报社的电话让退路不可能。烧掉的桥,把他的承诺成倍放大。
- **与目标一致的行动。** 一个牧师*讲道*。他用了自己手里仅有的工具 —— 讲台 —— 但他用到了极致。他没有等到自己发明一种新媒介。

## 我们能学到什么 —— 尤其是关于致富

Gunsaulus 故事是《Think and Grow Rich》里最干净的一个例证:**带日期地做出决定,常常能解锁多年许愿都拿不到的资源**。两年的思考产出为零。一个决定加一个截止日期加一次公开承诺,产出了 **36 小时内的一百万美元**。分子几乎没变;分母全变了。瓶颈从来不是梦想。瓶颈是"做决定"这个动作。

第二条同样可携带的教训是,**在决定"什么"和"什么时候"之前,你不需要先知道"怎么做"**。Gunsaulus 打电话给报社时根本没有募款方案。"怎么做"是在决定之后才到达的,而不是之前。多数人把这件事颠倒过来 —— 他们在不知道怎么做之前拒绝决定 —— 所以他们永远做不出决定。

第三条:**使用你已经掌握的媒介。** Gunsaulus 是个牧师,他就讲道。他没有等自己先创办一家杂志、雇一位募款顾问、组建一个董事会。他抓起手里已有的工具,用到了极致。现代翻译完全对应:用你已经在日历上的那场布道、那段代码、那篇文章、那通销售电话、那次会议 —— 把它用到极致,你现有的媒介几乎总是足够的。

第四条:**用公开承诺烧桥。** 通知报社不是营销,是心理学意义上的不可逆。一旦上了报,前路就只剩两条:讲并成功,或讲并公开失败。"私下失败"这条菜单上没了。菜单这一点微妙的变化,正是驱动行为变化的东西。

第五条:**人们出资给愿景,不出资给请求。** Phillip D. Armour 不是因为 Gunsaulus *需要*一百万美元才开支票。他开支票,是因为那场布道讲的是有了一百万会去*做*什么。永远卖"用途",而不是"索要"。

### 今日致富的清单

- 决定要带截止日期。决定 + 日期 + 公开承诺,是任何人手里杠杆最高的单一动作。
- 在决定"什么"之前别再试图先知道"怎么做"。"怎么做"是在决定之后到达的,而不是之前。**Hill 的教训是:钱在 36 小时内到达(within 36 HOURS)。**
- 使用你已经掌握的媒介。别等自己发明一种新的 —— 把那场布道讲了、把那段代码发了、把那篇文章写了、把那场会开了。
- 用"公开"烧一座小桥。告诉同行、客户、团队。退路在心理上会变得很贵。
- 完全清楚地说出:你将拿这笔钱、这个角色、这家公司*做什么*。人们出资给愿景,不出资给请求。
- 把梦想孵化到"感觉自然"为止 —— 然后在一个周末里行动,而不是再用上两年。
